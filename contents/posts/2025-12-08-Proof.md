## Basic Proof
- **Vaild Argument**
  - An argument consists of premises and a conclusion
  - we can say argument is vaild when the truth of its premises guarantees the truth of its conclusion,this means conditional statement formed from the priemises and the conclusion is a tautology.
  - state mathematically:(p₁∧p₂∧p₃...pₙ)→q is a tautology

- **Fallacy**
  - Fallacy of Affirming the Conclusion: A conclusion can be supported by multiple premises
  - Fallacy of Denying the Antecedent: The cause can be wrong,but the result can still be right
